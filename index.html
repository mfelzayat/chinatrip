🐉 Dragon Boat Festival · 端午节

Houhai Lake, Beijing · public holiday nationwide

You arrive in Beijing exactly on Duanwu — China's biggest summer festival. Dragon boat races on Houhai Lake from 09:00, zongzi (sticky rice parcels) sold everywhere, fragrant sachets handed out for luck. Streets are alive but landmarks may be packed; expect crowds at the Forbidden City the next day.

June 2026 · all 3 cities

🌧️ Plum Rain Season · 梅雨

Yangtze region — Shanghai especially

Late June is China's meiyu rainy season, especially in Shanghai. Daily highs 28-32°C, sticky humidity 75-85%, afternoon thunderstorms common. Pack collapsible umbrellas, ponchos, and quick-dry layers — refillable bottles and a fan are essential. Beijing & Xi'an drier; Shanghai-Disney days will need rain plans.

Pack accordingly Ponchos 28–32°C

Practical Tips · نصائح عملية

  • Currency: 1 EGP ≈ 0.14 CNY · exchange at airport or bank
  • No Google Maps in China — use Baidu Maps or download Google offline before landing
  • VPN needed for WhatsApp, Instagram, Gmail — install before you arrive
  • Payments: Carry cash CNY + set up Alipay/WeChat Pay (foreigner versions available since 2024)
  • SIM: Get a China data SIM at PEK airport on arrival (China Unicom tourist card)
  • Voltage: 220V / 50Hz — bring a Type A/C adapter (Chinese plugs)
  • Drinking water: Never drink tap water — stick to bottled
  • Western dining first — most luxury hotels (Marriott, Westin, Sofitel, Peninsula, Disney resorts) have full Western kitchens. We default to those. Halal awareness: seafood/fish/vegetarian Western dishes are always safe; clarify "no pork/no alcohol in cooking" politely.
  • Halal labelling: 清真 (Qīngzhēn) signs mark certified halal restaurants — useful for backup, not the main plan.
  • Tipping: Not customary in China · service charge often included at luxury hotels
  • Emergency: Dial 110 (police) · 120 (ambulance) · 119 (fire)

Getting Around · المواصلات

  • Beijing — Metro is best (¥3–9/ride · English signage). Didi for night/airport. Avoid hailing taxis on the street unless metered.
  • Xi'an — Compact center · Metro Line 2 connects city wall + bell tower. Didi cheap (¥15–30 most rides). Bike rental on city wall ¥45/3hr.
  • Shanghai — Metro is excellent (Line 11 to Disney from Hongqiao or Pudong via transfer). Didi widely available. Maglev from Pudong airport (¥50, 8 min, 431 km/h).
  • HSR (high-speed rail) — faster than flying for <1,500 km. Book via Trip.com (English) or 12306. Ticket sales open 15 days before departure. Bring passports — printed/PDF ticket scanned at gate.
  • Sleeper trains — soft sleeper = 4 berths/compartment with door. Deluxe soft sleeper = 2 berths + private bath (rare). Hard sleeper = 6 open berths (skip with kids).
  • Airport transfers — pre-book via hotel or Didi Premium. Allow 90 min for Beijing PEK · 60 min for Xi'an XIY · 90 min for Shanghai PVG.
  • Walking/lockable — keep cash in 2 pockets · zipper-secure passports · pickpockets target tourists at major sites.
